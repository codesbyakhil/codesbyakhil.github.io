DNS · Multiphase flows · HPC & GPU computing

Akhil S. Pillai

Project Associate, Department of Aerospace Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Madras — working with Prof. A. Sameen.

I work on direct numerical simulation of turbulent air–water flow past a circular cylinder at Re = 100–300, using an in-house compressible six-equation two-fluid solver written in Fortran. The runs use meshes of 8–30 million cells, distributed across GPUs on PARAM Shakti with one GPU per MPI rank. At the moment I am validating shedding frequencies against published Strouhal-number data and studying how the gas–liquid interface behaves in the wake.
